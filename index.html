EAGLE
Eagles are large birds of prey with powerful beaks and talons that are known for their strength and ferocity:
Size: Eagles can weigh up to 7 kg and have a wingspan of up to 2.4 m. Appearance: Eagles have fully feathered heads, often with crests, and strong feet with curved talons.

OWL
Most owls have huge heads, stocky bodies, soft feathers, short tails, and a reversible toe that can point either forward or backward. Owl's eyes face forward, like humans do. Most owl species are active at night, not in the daytime. There are about 250 species of owls in the world.

CROW
American Crows are familiar over much of the continent:Crows are large birds with long legs, thick necks, and heavy bills. They are about twice the size of a blue jay and about two-thirds the size of a raven. large, intelligent, all-black birds with hoarse, cawing voices. They are common sights in treetops, fields, and roadsides, and in habitats ranging from open woods and empty beaches to town centers.
